Call for Papers

AI Discovery in the Wild invites paper submissions on methods, systems, evaluation, and deployment for AI discovery beyond benchmark settings.

Paper Template Back to Main Page
01

Submission formats

4-Page Papers

  • Length: 4 content pages, excluding references.
  • Uses the same paper template and formatting rules as the 9-page format.
  • Suitable for concise new ideas, focused evaluations, negative results, and tightly scoped systems or deployment studies.

9-Page Papers

  • Length: 9 content pages, excluding references.
  • This is the full-format submission option for the workshop.
  • Suitable for mature systems work, broader empirical studies, and detailed deployment or infrastructure papers.
02

Format and template

Format

  • 9-page papers match the official NeurIPS 2026 paper format: same template, same page geometry, and the same 9-page main-text limit.
  • 4-page papers use the same template and formatting, but with a 4-page main-text limit.
  • Submissions should be uploaded as PDF files using the official paper template.
  • The paper should include a concise abstract, main text, and references.
  • Appendices are allowed, but reviewers are not required to read them.
  • Figures, tables, and system diagrams should be legible in the final review PDF.

Template

Official paper template
  • Use the official template package from the conference website.
  • For submission, authors should prepare an anonymous review version.
  • Workshop submission portal: TBD
03

Policies

Double-Blind Review

  • All submissions are double blind. Author names and affiliations should be omitted from the review copy.
  • Where possible, code repositories, websites, and artifacts should be anonymized for review.
  • References to prior work should follow standard anonymous-submission practice.

Non-Archival

  • This workshop is intended to be non-archival and does not plan formal proceedings.
  • Submissions under review elsewhere, recently submitted conference papers, and previously posted preprints are welcome.
  • If the work is under review or previously posted, authors should disclose that status at submission time.
04

Dates and review

Key Dates

  • Submission deadline: April 12, 2026
  • Notification: May 5, 2026
  • Final materials: May 15, 2026
  • Workshop day: May 26, 2026
  • Submission portal: TBD

Decisions and Presentation

  • Each submission will receive at least two reviews from the program committee.
  • Reviews will evaluate relevance, technical soundness, clarity, novelty, and discussion value.
  • Accepted papers will be included in the workshop program and may be selected for spotlight talks.
  • All accepted papers will be presented in poster form at the workshop.
  • A Best Paper Award will recognize one especially strong submission.
