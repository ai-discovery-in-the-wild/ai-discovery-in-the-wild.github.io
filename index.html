Overview

AI systems can now search over code, experiments, and workflows to produce candidate discoveries. This workshop focuses on agents that move beyond benchmark settings and operate under expensive evaluations, noisy measurements, underspecified objectives, and deployment constraints.

We invite work on methods, evaluation, systems, and deployment for discovery agents in science, engineering, and other real-world settings where reliable validation and human oversight matter.

Evaluation

How should discovery systems be assessed when reliable ground truth is limited or unavailable?

Human Oversight

Where should domain experts remain in the loop during search, validation, and deployment?

Systems

What infrastructure is needed to run iterative discovery workflows reliably at scale?

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions across the lifecycle of AI agents for discovery. The list below is intended to clarify scope rather than restrict it.

  1. Agentic and Evolutionary Systems

    Design, architecture, and evaluation of agents and evolutionary methods for discovery and optimization.

  2. LLM-Driven Search and Optimization

    Search over code, algorithms, configurations, and designs, including self-refinement and hybrid approaches.

  3. Discovery Problems and Evaluation

    Problem formulation for real settings with expensive simulations, noisy measurements, and multi-objective tradeoffs.

  4. Real-World Deployment

    Experience reports and case studies from production or applied settings, including both successes and failures.

  5. Infrastructure for Discovery Systems

    Reliability, reproducibility, cost management, and integration with existing workflows.

  6. Multi-Agent Systems

    Architectures and coordination strategies for collaborative agent systems working on discovery tasks.

  7. Reinforcement Learning

    RL for guiding search, reward design, policy learning, and exploration-exploitation tradeoffs.

  8. Human-AI Collaboration

    Interfaces between domain experts and autonomous discovery systems, including human-in-the-loop workflows.

  9. Safety and Verifiability

    Validation, safety, and trust for AI-generated solutions in domains without clean ground truth.

  10. Scaling, Cost, and Efficiency

    Tradeoffs between exploration depth, model cost, and solution quality across different frameworks.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions from researchers and practitioners working on autonomous AI systems for discovery and optimization, especially in settings that move beyond benchmark tasks to real-world deployment.

We especially welcome experience reports and case studies from deployed systems, including both successes and failures.

Submission details

  • Formats: 4-page short papers and 9-page long papers.
  • File format: A single PDF submission, up to 50 MB.
  • Template: Official paper template.
  • Length: References and appendices do not count toward the page limit, but the main text should be self-contained.
  • Review: Double blind, with at least two reviews per submission.
  • Criteria: Reviews will consider relevance, technical quality, novelty, and discussion potential.
  • Submission site: OpenReview portal coming soon.
  • Presentation: All accepted papers will be presented as posters, and a small number will be selected for oral presentations.
  • Award: The workshop will present a Best Paper Award.

Policies

Anonymized review

Review PDFs must be anonymized, including supplementary or linked material.

Non-archival

Concurrent submissions and recently accepted work are welcome if venue policies permit.

Visibility

Submissions and reviews will not be public; only accepted papers will be made public.
