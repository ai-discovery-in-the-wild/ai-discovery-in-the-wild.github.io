About

Recent advances in LLM-driven optimization have established a powerful class of AI discovery systems, including AlphaEvolve, GEPA, SkyDiscover, OpenEvolve, Glia, Claude Code, and Cursor, that autonomously generate, evaluate, and refine solutions.

While these results are striking, they predominantly originate from well-defined benchmark settings. The practical challenges change quickly once discovery systems face expensive simulation loops, noisy measurements, underspecified objectives, and deployment constraints.

This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners to chart a roadmap for AI discovery engines capable of producing novel and trustworthy results in the wild.

Primary Focus

We are especially interested in settings where evaluation is costly, feedback is imperfect, and human judgment still matters for validation, safety, and operational adoption.

Key Questions
How should discovery systems be evaluated when reliable ground truth is unavailable?
Where should experts stay in the loop during search, validation, and deployment?
What infrastructure is needed to run iterative discovery workflows reliably at scale?

Themes

Six discussion tracks spanning systems, optimization, reliability, and deployment.

I
Problem Formulation and Evaluation

Expensive simulations, noisy measurements, and multi-objective tradeoffs.

II
Scaling, Cost, and Efficiency

Exploration depth, model spend, and solution quality under real constraints.

III
Trust and Validation

How to validate AI-discovered outputs when clear ground truth is unavailable.

IV
Human-AI Collaboration

Interfaces between domain experts, search systems, and evaluation pipelines.

V
Systems Infrastructure

Reproducible, observable, and reliable execution of discovery workflows at scale.

VI
Case Studies

Successes, failures, and lessons from real deployment environments.

02

Call for Papers

The workshop accepts paper submissions on AI discovery beyond toy benchmarks, including methods, systems, evaluation, and real-world deployment studies.

Topics

Relevant submissions may be methodological, empirical, systems-oriented, or deployment-driven, but they should speak clearly to discovery under real constraints.

  • Agentic and evolutionary systems for discovery
  • LLM-driven search and optimization
  • Discovery problems and evaluation methodologies
  • Real-world deployment of discovery systems
  • Infrastructure for large-scale discovery workflows
  • Multi-agent systems for scientific and systems discovery
  • Human-AI collaborative discovery interfaces
  • Safety, verifiability, and trust in generated solutions
